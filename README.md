# [rpshr](https://rpshr.app)

A better way to journal your workouts.

Track your lifts, progressive overload, and progress. Add a new lift, and include your PR. Add a set. Update
your PR when you break it. Keep getting stronger.

Focused on simplicity, keyboard navigation, accessibility, and ease-of-use. Has limited features, but does
what it does well.

Might add more, might not.

## how

**Add a lift**

To begin, add a new lift to your account by inputing the name of the lift, your personal record (PR) for that lift, as `lbs` or `kgs`, as well as the date you set that PR. The date can be formatted as `Today`, `Yesterday`, or `MM/DD/YYYY`, and rpshr will normalize it for you.

**Add a set**

To add a workout for the lift, click on the lift you want to update, and input detail as `sets` `reps` `weight` `lbs` or `kgs`, and the `date` of the workout. You can optionally include a short note as well.

The date can be formatted as `Today`, `Yesterday`, or `MM/DD/YYYY`, and rpshr will normalize it for you. If the weight of your workout is higher than the lift's current PR, rpshr will automatically update it for you.

**Roadmap**

- Allow users to input multiple sets/reps as a part of the single string, i.e. `5 5 245lbs, 3 5 255lbs, 1 5 265lbs, Today, Felt great`.
- Allow users to input the weight as a percentage of their PR and automatically calculate it, i.e. `5 5 80%, Today, Felt great`.
